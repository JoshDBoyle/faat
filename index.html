Harley Boyle

Harley H. Boyle joined GE Aviation as an X-ray Technician reviewing aerospace raw material from several manufacturers. He held that position until being promoted to a higher level and in this position he established techniques to internally evaluate critical aerospace hardware. In addition, he also evaluated film and identified the areas that required repair and subsequent correction. Having held this position for nine years he was subsequently promoted to a supervisory position overseeing the assignment of work for fifteen X-ray Technicians covering a three shift operation.

Later, a position within the Quality department was needed in one of the major fabricated manufacturing areas. He applied and was accepted. He remained in that billet for two years and at that time he accepted a position in the Largest Fabricated Manufacturing area.

After being in that position for three years he moved once again into Supplier Purchased Material Quality and had responsibility to oversee Supplier Hardware. He remained in that position for many years providing Quality Support to several different Suppliers of various types of hardware from Major Fabricated Components to Configuration Hardware.

His last nine years in C-Parts Quality were spent Managing six Product Quality and three Manufacturing Engineers who had responsibility for approximately 130 Aerospace Suppliers both in the USA as well as internationally. Upon his retirement he had spent his entire career in the aerospace industry.

Lynn Alexander

Lynn B. Alexander is a retired Air Force Colonel and General Electric Aircraft Engines retiree. He received his Masters Degree in Industrial Management from Central Michigan University. After twenty-four years of several leadership roles with the Air Force, he retired and entered the commercial world with General Electric Aircraft Engines. His experience ranges from Configuration Management of Space vehicles, instructor pilot, production, Air Force leader of Program Depot Level Maintenance of Presidential aircraft, Procurement and Director of Contracting.

In civilian life, he was manager of Sourcing Administration, Aircraft engine supplies and equipment procurement and engine spare parts customer service. After retirement, Lynn became very active as an instructor in gliders.

John Conover

John Conover is a former U.S. Marine who obtained the rank of Captain while serving in Aviation supply and as an Infantry Officer. John received his Bachelor’s degree in Business Administration from Morehead State University in Morehead, KY. He was a 30 year employee of GE where his specialties were quality, compliance and auditing.

Positions held at GE included:

  • Manager of Quality Assurance and Auditing
  • Policy Compliance Manager
  • Senior Product Quality Engineer

John also received the President’s Award and GE Management Award from GE Aircraft.

John’s areas of expertise include:

  • ISO Standard 17025/ANSI/Z540
  • ISO 13485/ISO 9001
  • ASQ-Certified Quality Auditor-2007
  • FAA Orders and Directives

Currently, John is an Audit and Quality/Manufacturing Systems consultant and Quality Auditor

Paul Faessler

Paul Faessler, a Cincinnati native, received his BS in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Cincinnati in 1980. Ford was not hiring at the time, so he accepted a position as an engineer for General Motors. After three years, he declined a promotion to Central Office, and decided to start his own business doing what he always wanted to do: working on Mustangs and Fords.

Paul’s business started in his four-car garage at home, restoring Mustangs. After three years he realized he needed a larger facility to keep up with his customers’ needs. He moved into the current facility, initially renting a small portion, and eventually purchasing the building. He then expanded his operation, using the entire 18,000 square foot space.

Years Of Dedication
30 years of Paul’s consistent dedication to high standards have produced excellent results. He has completed numerous 100 point concourse Shelby and Mustang restorations, winning over 200 MCA and SAAC National Gold awards.

Proven Performance
His road racing designs and preparations have won 8 NASA American Iron/American Iron Extreme national championships. He has built plenty of drag racers, including the fastest NMRA S.S. Outlaw in the country running 7.71 seconds at 193 m.p.h. His projects have also included late model street performance cars (over 50 magazine feature builds including Hot Rod Magazine).
